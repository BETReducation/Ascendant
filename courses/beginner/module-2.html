🏦 What is a crypto exchange?

An exchange is a platform where you can buy, sell, and hold crypto using regular money (like £GBP, $USD, or €EUR). Think of it like a bureau de change at an airport — except it's online and open 24/7.

When you buy ADA on an exchange, the exchange holds it on your behalf. You don't actually own it yet — not in the crypto sense. The exchange holds the keys. This is called custodial ownership, and it's an important distinction we'll cover in Module 3.

When you buy on an exchange, your ADA sits in their wallet — not yours. We'll fix that in Module 4.

🔍 Choosing a reputable exchange

Not all exchanges are equal. Some have been hacked, gone bankrupt, or frozen withdrawals. Here's what to look for:

What to look for in an exchange
1
Regulated & licensed — look for exchanges that are registered with financial regulators in your country (e.g. FCA in the UK, FinCEN in the US). This provides basic consumer protections.
2
Proof of Reserves — reputable exchanges publish cryptographic proof that they hold the assets they claim to. Avoid any that don't.
3
Supports ADA withdrawals — some exchanges only let you trade, not withdraw to your own wallet. Always check before depositing.
4
Two-factor authentication (2FA) — your account should require a second verification step beyond just a password. Use an authenticator app (not SMS if possible).
5
Clear fee structure — check trading fees, withdrawal fees, and any deposit fees upfront. Hidden fees add up quickly.
⚠️

Remember FTX: In 2022, one of the world's largest exchanges (FTX) collapsed overnight and customers lost billions. This is why we always recommend moving ADA to your own wallet — covered in Modules 3 & 4.

🛒 How the buying process works

Every exchange is slightly different, but the general flow is the same. Here's what to expect:

exchange.example.com/buy
Step 1 — Create & verify your account
You'll need to provide your email, create a password, and verify your identity (passport or driving licence). This is called KYC — Know Your Customer. It's a legal requirement on regulated exchanges.
Step 2 — Deposit funds
Transfer money from your bank account. Bank transfer is usually cheapest (often free). Card payments are faster but carry a 1–3% fee.
Step 3 — Buy ADA
Search for "ADA" or "Cardano", enter the amount in your local currency, review the fees, and confirm. Your ADA will appear in your exchange balance within seconds.

Step 4 — Enable 2FA immediately
Before doing anything else, set up two-factor authentication. Go to account security settings → enable authenticator app. Don't skip this.

⚖️ Pros & cons of keeping ADA on an exchange

Buying on an exchange is the starting point — but it comes with trade-offs you should understand before deciding how long to leave your ADA there.

✓ Advantages

  • Easy to buy and sell quickly
  • No wallet setup required
  • Price charts and tools built in
  • Customer support if you're locked out
  • Can set up recurring purchases

✗ Risks

  • Exchange holds your keys — not you
  • Exchanges can be hacked or go bankrupt
  • Withdrawals can be frozen without notice
  • You can't use ADA in dApps or DeFi
  • Can't stake natively (some offer custodial staking)
