🚀 Why move ADA to your own wallet?

Moving ADA to your own wallet completes the journey to true self-custody.

✅ Before you send — the safety checklist

Crypto transactions are irreversible. If you send ADA to the wrong address, it's gone. Take these steps before sending a single lovelace:

Pre-send checklist
1
Have your receiving address ready — open your Lace wallet and go to Receive. Copy the full address. Cardano addresses start with addr1 and are 59–103 characters long.
2
Send a test transaction first — before sending your full balance, send a small amount (e.g. 5–10 ADA). Verify it arrives in your wallet. Then send the rest. This small precaution has saved people thousands.
3
Select the correct network — make sure you're sending on the Cardano network, not ERC-20 or another chain. Sending Cardano ADA to an Ethereum address will result in permanent loss.
4
Check the withdrawal fee — your exchange will charge a small network/withdrawal fee (typically 0.5–2 ADA). Make sure you have enough balance to cover this on top of your send amount.
5
Double-check the address before confirming — compare the first 6 and last 6 characters of the address you pasted with the one in your wallet. Clipboard-hijacking malware can swap addresses silently.

How long does it take? Cardano transactions typically confirm in 20–60 seconds on-chain. However, exchanges often have internal processing queues that add 5–30 minutes. Your test transaction should arrive within the hour.

🔍 Verifying your ADA arrived

Once you've sent the transaction, here's how to confirm it landed:

Checking your transaction
1
Check your Lace wallet — open the wallet extension and your balance should update automatically. If it doesn't update, try refreshing the page.
2
Verify on Cardanoscan — visit cardanoscan.io and paste your wallet address. You'll see every transaction in and out, with block confirmations and timestamps. This is public blockchain data — no login needed.
3
Confirm your test, then send the rest — once the test amount (e.g. 10 ADA) appears in your wallet and on Cardanoscan, go back to the exchange and withdraw the remaining balance.

🎯 What to do next

With ADA in your own wallet, you've achieved real self-custody. Here's what opens up for you now:

✓ You can now

  • Stake your ADA and earn ~3% annual rewards
  • Keep staking rewards liquid — no lock-up
  • Use Cardano dApps and DeFi protocols
  • Buy, sell, and hold Cardano NFTs
  • Participate in Cardano governance (Voltaire)
  • Upgrade to a hardware wallet for extra security

✗ Keep in mind

  • You are now solely responsible for security
  • Guard your seed phrase with your life
  • Be alert to phishing and fake dApp sites
  • Only connect your wallet to trusted dApps
  • ADA price remains volatile — invest carefully
