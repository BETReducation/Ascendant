5 rules to keep your ADA safe

The five habits that protect your ADA from scams, hacks, and mistakes. Read these once and remember them forever.

⏱ 3 min 🔐 No technical knowledge needed ⚠️ This one really matters
Home › Courses › Essentials › Stay safe
🛡️ Goal: Protect your ADA from the most common threats
📖 Format: Five rules — read them, remember them
⚠️ Reality: Most ADA losses are from scams, not hacks
1

Rule 1 — Never share your seed phrase with anyone. Ever.

Your 24-word seed phrase is the master key to your wallet. Anyone who has it can take every ADA you own — instantly and permanently. There is no recovery once it's gone.

🚫

No legitimate service ever asks for your seed phrase. Not Lace support. Not Cardano Foundation. Not Ascendant. Not a "wallet verifier" on Discord or Twitter. If someone asks for it, it is a scam — 100% of the time.

Rule 2 — Always type wallet URLs directly. Never click links.

Scammers create fake versions of popular crypto sites (Lace, Minswap, fractal.is) that look pixel-perfect. When you connect your wallet to a fake site and sign a transaction, your funds can be drained instantly.

  • ✅ Type lace.io directly into your browser
  • ✅ Bookmark the real sites you use regularly
  • 🚫 Never click wallet links in emails, DMs, or Discord
  • 🚫 Never click ads in Google — they may be fake sites
📸 Example image: Browser address bar showing a fake vs real URL Side by side — lace.io (real) vs lace-wallet-app.io (fake)
3

Rule 3 — Read what you're signing before confirming

When you connect your wallet to a dApp and click confirm, you're signing a transaction. Always check what the transaction says before approving. Lace shows you a summary — read it.

  • ✅ Check the ADA amount matches what you expect
  • ✅ Check it's going to the address you intend
  • 🚫 Never sign a transaction that sends more ADA than you expected
  • 🚫 Never sign a transaction on a site you don't trust
📸 Screenshot: Lace transaction confirmation dialog Show the transaction details screen with amount, recipient, and fee visible
4

Rule 4 — Use 2FA on every exchange and email account

Even if your wallet is secure, your exchange account can be compromised. And if your email is compromised, attackers can reset passwords everywhere. Protect both with two-factor authentication.

💡 Use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator or Authy) rather than SMS. SIM-swapping attacks can intercept SMS codes.
📸 Screenshot: Google Authenticator app showing a 6-digit code Show the authenticator app with a rotating code — no real account details needed
5

Rule 5 — If it sounds too good to be true, it is

The most common crypto scam is the "send X ADA and receive 2X back" giveaway. It is always a scam. No exceptions. The same applies to guaranteed returns, "recovery services" for lost wallets, and unsolicited investment advice from strangers online.

🚨

Common scams to know: Fake giveaways · Fake support staff on Twitter/Discord · Romance scams leading to "investment opportunities" · Fake wallet recovery services · "Rug pulls" — projects that take investor money and disappear.

📸 Example image: Screenshot of a common "ADA giveaway" scam tweet Annotated to highlight the warning signs — fake account, too-good offer, urgency language
✅

The golden rule: If you are ever unsure whether something is a scam, assume it is. The cost of being wrong is zero. The cost of being scammed can be everything.
