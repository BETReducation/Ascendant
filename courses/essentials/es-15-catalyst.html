Submit a Catalyst proposal

Cardano's treasury funds real projects. Here's how to apply — from picking a template to hitting submit before the deadline.

📋 What you'll do: Understand the Catalyst process and submit a proposal

💡

What is Catalyst? Project Catalyst is Cardano's community-governed funding program. Each round, a portion of the Cardano treasury is allocated to proposals voted on by ADA holders. Over $100M has been distributed to projects around the world since 2020.

1

Check if a funding round is open

Go to projectcatalyst.io and check the current status. Catalyst runs in funding rounds — typically a few per year. If a round is open, you'll see a submission deadline on the homepage. Sign up for their newsletter to be notified of future rounds.

📸 Screenshot: projectcatalyst.io homepage Show the homepage with current fund status and submission deadline if open
2

Read the current challenge brief

Each funding round has categories (challenges) — e.g. "Developer ecosystem", "Open source", "Community events". Read the briefs carefully and identify which category your project fits best. Your proposal must address the specific challenge to be considered.

📸 Screenshot: Catalyst — Challenge categories list Show the funding categories with descriptions and budget allocations
3

Fill in the proposal form

Create an account on projectcatalyst.io and click Submit Proposal. The form will ask for:

  • Title — short, clear, specific (e.g. "Open-source Cardano wallet SDK for React Native")
  • Problem — what specific problem does your project solve?
  • Solution — what exactly will you build or do?
  • Impact — how does this benefit the Cardano ecosystem?
  • Budget breakdown — itemised costs in USD (grants are paid in ADA)
  • Team — who is building this, and why are they qualified?
  • Milestones — specific, measurable deliverables tied to funding tranches
📸 Screenshot: Catalyst proposal form Show the proposal editor with the key fields — title, problem, solution visible
4

Tips that get proposals funded

  • ✅ Be specific — vague proposals don't get funded. "Build a better wallet" fails. "Add multi-sig support to Eternl for teams" succeeds.
  • ✅ Break your budget down — reviewers distrust lump-sum requests. Show your working.
  • ✅ Link to prior work — GitHub repos, previous projects, track record. Trust is everything.
  • ✅ Keep milestones small and measurable — "Month 1: working prototype with X feature deployed to testnet" is good. "Build the thing" is not.
  • 🚫 Don't pad the budget — reviewers know market rates. Inflated requests are regularly flagged.
  • 🚫 Don't submit in the last hour — leave time for review and edits.
5

Submit and engage with the community

Click Submit before the deadline. Your proposal becomes publicly visible. Engage with comments and questions from the community — proposals with active authors consistently score higher in reviews.

After the submission period closes, Community Reviewers score proposals. Then ADA holders vote. If you pass the threshold, funding is released in milestones as you deliver.

✅

You've submitted your Catalyst proposal. Even if it's not funded in your first round, the feedback from reviewers is invaluable — many successful projects were rejected once before being funded.
