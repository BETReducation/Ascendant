What a candlestick tells you

Each candle represents a time period (1 minute, 1 hour, 1 day — your choice). It tells you four things about the price during that period:

  • Open — the price when the period started
  • Close — the price when the period ended
  • High — the highest price reached
  • Low — the lowest price reached

A green candle means the price went up during that period. A red candle means it went down.

Where to view ADA/USD charts

Go to tradingview.com and search for ADA/USD or ADAUSDT. This gives you a professional chart you can zoom, pan, and switch timeframes on. Alternatively, most exchanges (Kraken, Coinbase) show charts directly on their trading pages.

3

Switch timeframes to zoom in and out

The timeframe selector (usually top left — 1D, 4H, 1H, 15m etc.) changes what each candle represents. As a beginner:

  • 1D (daily) — use this for the big picture. Is the trend going up or down over weeks and months?
  • 4H (4 hour) — medium term. Useful if you're thinking about entering or exiting a position.
  • 1H or 15m — short term noise. Mostly useful for professional traders — ignore as a beginner.
💡 If the daily chart shows a clear downtrend (lower highs, lower lows), the shorter timeframes showing green candles can be misleading. Always check the bigger picture first.
4

Check the volume bars at the bottom

Below the candles you'll see vertical bars — this is volume (how much ADA was traded during each candle period). Volume confirms whether a price move is significant:

  • Big price move + high volume = the move is real and significant
  • Big price move + low volume = treat with caution, could reverse
  • Flat price + low volume = the market is undecided

5

What NOT to pay attention to

Charts attract a huge amount of noise. As a beginner, ignore:

  • 🚫 Price predictions on social media — anyone can draw an arrow going up
  • 🚫 Complex indicators (RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands) — not needed at this stage
  • 🚫 Very short timeframes (1m, 5m) — pure noise for anyone who isn't a professional trader
  • 🚫 "This will go 100x" calls — if someone knows this, they wouldn't be posting it publicly
⚠️

Charts show you what has happened. They cannot reliably predict what will happen next. Use them to understand context and trend direction — not as a signal to buy or sell.
